 What is Heart Disease?
Heart disease refers to a group of conditions that affect the heart's structure and function. The most common type is coronary artery disease (CAD), which occurs when the arteries supplying blood to the heart become hardened and narrowed due to plaque buildup (atherosclerosis).

⚠️ Common Risk Factors
High blood pressure (Hypertension)

High cholesterol

Smoking

Obesity

Diabetes

Family history of heart disease

Sedentary lifestyle

Unhealthy diet

📉 Symptoms of Heart Disease
Chest pain or discomfort (angina)

Shortness of breath

Fatigue

Irregular heartbeat

Dizziness or lightheadedness

Swelling in legs or abdomen (in advanced stages)

🧪 Diagnostic Methods
Electrocardiogram (ECG) – measures electrical activity of the heart

Echocardiogram – ultrasound image of the heart

Stress test – checks how the heart performs under stress

Cardiac catheterization – checks for blocked arteries using dye

Blood tests – check cholesterol and enzyme levels

💊 Treatment Options
Lifestyle changes: diet, exercise, quitting smoking

Medications: beta-blockers, statins, ACE inhibitors, antiplatelet drugs

Medical procedures: angioplasty, stent placement, bypass surgery

Cardiac rehabilitation: supervised exercise + lifestyle training

✅ Prevention Tips
Eat heart-healthy food (low sodium, low saturated fats)

Exercise regularly (at least 30 minutes/day)

Manage stress

Avoid tobacco and limit alcohol

Monitor blood pressure and cholesterol

Control diabetes effectively
